Question 2:


If some features are highly correlated or duplicated 
(multicollinearity), what mathematical problem can 
occur in the Normal Equation?
Why does this happen?
How can it be solved without simply removing features 
or losing useful information?

--> 1. The Problem:

The issue is the inability to compute the inverse of the matrix
(X^T X)^−1
because the matrix becomes singular or non-invertible

2. Why does this happen?

This occurs because:

. When features are highly correlated or duplicated, the rows or columns of the matrix become linearly dependent.
. As a result, the determinant of the matrix becomes zero, which makes it non-invertible.

3. How can we solve the problem without removing any features?

The most common and effective solution is to use the (Moore–Penrose Pseudoinverse),Instead of computing:
θ=(X^T X)^−1 X^T y
We compute:
θ=X^+ y
Where:
X^+
is the pseudo-inverse, which is calculated using Singular Value Decomposition (SVD).
